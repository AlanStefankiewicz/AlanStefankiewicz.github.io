Razer Gaming Gear

BUY RAZER GEAR ON AMAZON

Gaming Gear

Personally, I prefer to spend more on the product which will work for years than buy a cheap one and replace it after a few months because it broke down.
There are several top gaming gear brands like Logitech, Corsair and other, but I'm such a fan of Razer.
All Razer gear I had was well made, serve me for years. Here are my thoughts about razer gear...

BUY RAZER NAGA ON AMAZON

Razer Gaming Mouse - Razer Naga

I have 2012 model of Razer Naga for 5 years now and it's not available anymore, but you can get the new version of this mouse with interchangeable side plates - 2, 7, and 12 button configuration.
It's the best mouse I've ever had. Having many buttons on the mouse is very useful in many RPG and shooter games, but I used to use just 9 of 12 buttons available.

Advantages:
- reliable
- has a lot of programmable buttons
- durable, tangle free cable
Disadvantages:
- top paint started to come off after 4 years of use
- left button started to double click sometimes after 5 years of use
- expensive

Razer Keyboard - Razer Anansi

Unfortunately, this keyboard is not available anymore, with 12 programmable buttons and not that big price it was the best options 6 years ago when I bought it.
It was working great before I spilled tea on it after that, I had to disable some programmable buttons. I may get a waterproof keyboard next time...
Currently, I was able to find just one Razer keyboard with extra programmable buttons - Razer BlackWidow Chroma V2 and it's very expensive.
If you don't need extra buttons on the side Razer has also much cheaper keyboards with all programmable buttons like Razer Cynosa Chroma or Razer Deathstalker.
Although I happened to use programmable buttons, they are not necessary for me and I will definitely buy cheaper Razer keyboard next time.

My personal choice now will be Razer Cynosa Chroma

Advantages:
- small
- spill-resistant
- affordable price
- individually programmable backlit keys

I can't tell much more because I don't own this keyboard yet.

Razer Keypads

I had Razer Nostromo and Orbweaver keypads and it's a great device if you keep playing one game every day. There was a time when I used to play only League of Legends and the keypad was a great
and comfortable device, but when I started to play multiple games I keep forgetting how the keypad buttons were configured on different profiles. I am not using it anymore.

The same keypads as mine are not available anymore, here you can find the new Razer keypads: RAZER KEYPADS ON AMAZON

Advantages:
- reliable
- has a lot of programmable buttons
- small and comfortable
Disadvantages:
- hard to remember buttons configuration if you play multiple games
- expensive

Razer Headsets

I had Razer Megalodon and Tiamat, I can tell these headphones have a great audio quality but the ear pads foam is not the best and doesn't look nice after time. If you wear glasses they may be not comfortable.
Moreover, the headband may break if you drop them on the floor too many times.

Currently, I use the old version of Razer Tiamat headphones which is not available anymore. Here you can find Razer Tiamat V2: RAZER TIAMAT 7.1 V2 ANALOG 7.1

Advantages:
- good sound quality
- audio control unit
- 7.1/stereo toggle button
- mute button
- microphone mute button
- speakers/headphones toggle button
- speakers output
- tangle free cable
Disadvantages:
- easy to break when dropped
- expensive
- bad ear pads quality

As always, a piece of tape comes to help.
